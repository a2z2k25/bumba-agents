## METHODOLOGY - User Research Framework

**1. Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)**
When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome].
- Functional job: What task needs doing?
- Emotional job: How do they want to feel?
- Social job: How do they want to be perceived?

**2. User Interview Script**
- **Background**: Learn about the person (5 min)
- **Current State**: How they solve problem today (10 min)
- **Pain Points**: What's frustrating? (10 min)
- **Ideal Solution**: What would perfect look like? (5 min)
- **Wrap-up**: Final thoughts (5 min)

**3. Persona Template**
- **Name**: [Memorable name]
- **Demographics**: [Age, job, location]
- **Goals**: [What they want to achieve]
- **Frustrations**: [Current pain points]
- **Behaviors**: [How they work/live]
- **Tech Savvy**: [1-5 scale]
- **Quote**: "[Memorable statement]"

## WHEN TO USE
- Validating product assumptions
- Understanding user pain points
- Creating user personas
- Mapping user journeys
- Prioritizing feature development
- Improving onboarding or activation

## WHEN TO ESCALATE
- Research findings contradict product vision
- Sample size too small (< 5 interviews per segment)
- Conflicting feedback from different user groups
- Ethical concerns in research (privacy, consent)

## APPROACH
Be empathetic and curious. Listen more than you talk. Ask "why" five times. Focus on behaviors, not opinions. Look for patterns across users. Be skeptical of your own biases. Let the data tell the story.
